Ocean Route Visualizer Module Instructions This module introduces the relationship between global shipping routes and whale migration patterns, providing users with the foundational knowledge needed to understand the broader impacts of maritime commerce on whale life. The module’s interactive map shows colour-coded shipping routes alongside major whale migration patterns. The various popup windows include research-based information and interesting facts about whale-vessel interactions; many of the facts are specific to the geographical region in which the popup window is located on the map. Several of the popup windows display true and false and multiple choice questions, allowing users to further interact with the content and helping to inform users of additional facts and information in an engaging manner. Users are encouraged to explore the map, click on the various information buttons, and learn about how whale life is constantly impacted by global maritime commerce. This module provides users with a solid understanding of the challenges whales experience during migration, ultimately establishing the foundational context that supports deeper reflection as users explore the rest of the application. The Routes Worldwide, there are five main channels through which the majority of shipping activities are observed. These routes include the English Channel, which connects the North Sea to the Atlantic Ocean, the Malacca Strait, located between Indonesia and the Malay Peninsula, the Magellan Strait and Chilean region, located at the southern point of Chile, the Hormouz Strait and Mozambique Channel, situated between Iran and Oman, and the Panama Canal, which links the Atlantic and Pacific oceans. Each of these channels and waterways hosts hundreds of vessels per day and simultaneously disrupts the feeding and migratory patterns of various species of whales; sperm whales, blue whales, and humpback whales are known to frequent the waters through which these major shipping channels flow.
